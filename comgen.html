First Generation (1940s-1950s)

The first electronic computers used vacuum tubes and were large, expensive, and unreliable.

Second Generation (1950s-1960s)

The use of transistors led to smaller, more reliable computers that were faster and more affordable.

Third Generation (1960s-1970s)

The use of integrated circuits made computers even smaller and more powerful, with improved processing speed and storage capacity.

Fourth Generation (1970s-1980s)

Microprocessors made it possible to create even smaller and more affordable computers, leading to the rise of personal computers.

Fifth Generation (1980s-1990s)

The development of artificial intelligence and parallel computing paved the way for supercomputers and advanced robotics.
